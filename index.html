Programme Objective

This FDP is designed to give faculty of the Marketing Management Department at DG Vaishnav College practical fluency in digital marketing — not textbook theory, but the tools, platforms, and frameworks that brands actually use today. By the end, every participant will be equipped to teach these concepts confidently and apply them in research and institution-building contexts.

Curriculum

Session-by-Session Breakdown

Day 01
Digital Marketing Foundations
2 Hours

How brands grow online. The full picture — from awareness to conversion — and why digital marketing is no longer optional for any business.

What is digital marketing and why it matters today
Traditional vs digital: the real difference
The marketing funnel: Awareness, Consideration, Conversion
Overview of all channels: SEO, Social, Paid, Email, Content
How brands plan and execute a digital strategy
The role of data in every marketing decision
Introduction to platforms and tools used in industry
Live brand analysis: mapping a real brand's digital footprint
Hands-On: Participants map a real brand's digital presence — channels used, funnel stages, and content types — and present key observations to the room.
Day 02
Website & Search Engine Optimisation
2 Hours

How to build and optimise a web presence that search engines rank and users trust. Covering the full SEO spectrum — on-page, off-page, and technical.

What makes a good website: structure, speed, and UX basics
Introduction to WordPress and how websites are built
What is SEO and how search engines work
On-page SEO: titles, meta descriptions, headings, alt text
Keyword research using Google Keyword Planner and SEMrush
Off-page SEO: backlinks and domain authority explained
Technical SEO: page speed, mobile-friendliness, sitemaps
Google Business Profile for local visibility
Hands-On: Run a free SEO audit on a live website using Google PageSpeed Insights. Then use Google Keyword Planner to find keywords for a topic of their choice.
Day 03
Social Media Marketing
2 Hours

How platforms work, what content wins, and how to build an audience that converts. Covering the major platforms with practical content creation exercises.

Platform overview: Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, Facebook
How algorithms decide what content gets shown
Content formats: Reels, Carousels, Stories, Long-form
Building a 30-day content calendar
Brand voice and visual identity on social media
Organic growth strategies: hashtags, engagement, community
Influencer marketing basics
Social media analytics: what to track and why
Hands-On: Build a 1-week content calendar for a mock brand. Write 3 captions — one for Instagram, one for LinkedIn, one as a Reel script — using Canva AI for visuals.
Day 04
Performance Marketing
2 Hours

How paid advertising works on Google and Meta — campaign structure, audience targeting, creative strategy, and the metrics that tell you whether it's working.

What is performance marketing vs brand marketing
Google Ads: Search, Display, YouTube — how each works
Campaign structure: Campaigns, Ad Sets, Ads explained
Audience targeting: demographics, interests, intent, lookalikes
Meta Ads (Facebook & Instagram): campaign walkthrough
Ad creatives: what drives people to click
Key metrics: CTR, CPM, CPC, ROAS, CAC — what they mean
Retargeting: how brands follow users across the internet
Hands-On: Live walkthrough of Meta Ads Manager. Participants structure a mock campaign — objective, audience, placements, and budget — for a hypothetical local business.
Day 05
Analytics & Reporting
2 Hours

How to read data, understand what's working, and make better decisions. Covering Google Analytics 4, social media insights, and how to build a simple marketing dashboard.

Introduction to Google Analytics 4 (GA4)
Key reports: Traffic sources, Audience, Behaviour, Conversions
What is a conversion and how to set one up
Bounce rate, session duration, and engagement rate explained
Social media analytics: Instagram Insights, LinkedIn Analytics
Building a simple marketing dashboard
Using data to justify marketing decisions
Monthly reporting template walkthrough
Hands-On: Explore a live GA4 demo account. Identify the top traffic source, best-performing page, and a conversion drop-off point. Fill in a 1-page marketing report template.
Day 06
AI for Teaching Digital Marketing
2 Hours

How faculty can use AI tools to teach digital marketing more effectively — building lesson plans, generating live examples, creating case studies, and making abstract concepts tangible for students.

Framework: The CRAFT Method for Teaching
C — Context: Set the teaching scenario for AI
R — Role: Assign AI the role of a student, brand, or expert
A — Ask: Request specific outputs (quiz, case study, plan)
F — Format: Define output format — table, paragraph, slides
T — Tune: Refine and iterate until classroom-ready
Live prompt-building exercise using ChatGPT and Claude
Tools Walkthrough
ChatGPT & Claude
Generate lesson plans, student assignments, case studies, and quiz questions for any digital marketing topic in minutes.
Canva AI
Build presentation slides, infographics, and visual teaching aids for concepts like the marketing funnel, ad formats, and analytics dashboards.
Perplexity AI
Prepare live, current examples and industry data to use in class — replacing outdated textbook case studies with real, recent brand stories.
Google NotebookLM
Upload course material, research papers, or syllabus documents and query them conversationally to build teaching notes and FAQs.
Gamma
Turn bullet-point notes into full presentation decks for lectures — with layouts, visuals, and examples auto-generated from a simple text prompt.
Hands-On: Each faculty member uses ChatGPT or Claude to generate a complete lesson plan for one digital marketing topic from the FDP — including objectives, case study, activities, and assessment questions. Ready to use in their next class.

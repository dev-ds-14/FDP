Curriculum
5 Sessions, Zero Filler

Each session is built around a core pillar of digital marketing. Topics are drawn directly from industry practice, not textbooks. Every session ends with a practical activity faculty can apply immediately.

01
Digital Marketing Foundations
How brands grow online. The full picture — from awareness to conversion.
Session 1 · ~90 min
Topics Covered
  • What is digital marketing and why it matters in 2025
  • Traditional vs digital: the real difference
  • The digital marketing funnel: Awareness, Consideration, Conversion
  • Overview of all channels: SEO, Social, Paid, Email, Content
  • How brands plan a digital strategy
  • Introduction to key tools and platforms
  • The role of data in every marketing decision
Practical Activity
Hands-On

Participants map out a real brand's digital presence — identifying which channels they use, how they move customers through the funnel, and where the gaps are. Discuss and present to the room.

02
Website & Search Engine Optimisation
How to build a web presence that Google finds — and people trust.
Session 2 · ~2 hrs
Topics Covered
  • What makes a good website — structure, speed, UX basics
  • Introduction to WordPress and how websites are built
  • What is SEO and how search engines work
  • On-page SEO: Title tags, meta descriptions, headings, alt text
  • Keyword research using Google Keyword Planner and SEMrush
  • Off-page SEO: Backlinks and domain authority explained simply
  • Technical SEO basics: page speed, mobile-friendliness, sitemaps
  • Google Business Profile setup for local visibility
  • What is AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) — the AI search era
Practical Activity
Hands-On

Participants run a free SEO audit on a live website using Google's PageSpeed Insights and identify the top 3 on-page fixes. Then use Google Keyword Planner to find keywords for a topic of their choice.

03
Social Media Marketing
How platforms work, what content wins, and how to build an audience that converts.
Session 3 · ~2 hrs
Topics Covered
  • Overview of platforms: Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, Facebook
  • How algorithms decide what content to show
  • Content formats: Reels, Carousels, Stories, Long-form
  • Content calendar: How to plan 30 days of posts
  • Brand voice and visual identity on social media
  • Organic growth strategies: hashtags, engagement, community
  • Influencer marketing basics
  • Social media analytics: what to track and why
  • AI tools for content creation (Canva AI, ChatGPT for captions)
Practical Activity
Hands-On

Participants build a 1-week content calendar for a mock brand using a Canva template and write 3 real captions — one for Instagram, one for LinkedIn, one for a Reel script. Peer review in pairs.

04
Performance Marketing
How paid advertising works — Google Ads, Meta Ads, and what makes a campaign successful.
Session 4 · ~2 hrs
Topics Covered
  • What is performance marketing and how it differs from branding
  • Google Ads: Search, Display, YouTube — how they work
  • Campaign structure: Campaigns, Ad Sets, Ads explained
  • Audience targeting: demographics, interests, intent, lookalikes
  • Meta Ads (Facebook & Instagram): setting up a campaign walkthrough
  • Ad creatives: what makes people click
  • Key metrics: CTR, CPM, CPC, ROAS, CAC — what they mean
  • Retargeting: how brands follow users across the internet
  • Small budget strategy: how to run ads for ₹500 and learn fast
Practical Activity
Hands-On

Live walkthrough of the Meta Ads Manager interface. Participants structure a mock campaign — choosing objective, audience, placements, and budget — for a hypothetical local business. No actual money spent.

05
Analytics & Reporting
How to read data, understand what's working, and make smarter decisions.
Session 5 · ~90 min
Topics Covered
  • Introduction to Google Analytics 4 (GA4)
  • Key reports: Traffic sources, Audience, Behaviour, Conversions
  • What is a conversion and how to set one up
  • Understanding bounce rate, session duration, and engagement rate
  • Social media analytics: Instagram Insights, LinkedIn Analytics
  • How to build a simple marketing dashboard
  • Using data to justify marketing decisions to management
  • Monthly reporting template walkthrough
Practical Activity
Hands-On

Participants explore a live GA4 demo account, identify the top traffic source, highest-performing page, and a conversion drop-off point. Then fill out a 1-page marketing report template with their findings.

★
Capstone Activity
Apply everything. Build a complete digital marketing plan for a real or mock business.
Final Session · ~60 min
What Participants Build
  • Choose a small business (local, familiar, or hypothetical)
  • Define the target audience and funnel
  • Pick 2 digital channels to focus on and explain why
  • Outline an SEO strategy and a social media content plan
  • Design a basic paid campaign structure with targeting
  • List 3 KPIs they would track and what success looks like
Format
Group Presentation

Teams of 3–4 present their digital marketing plan to the room. Trainer provides live feedback. This is not assessed — it is designed to consolidate learning and build confidence in applying the concepts taught.
